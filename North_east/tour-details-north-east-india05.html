Tour Plan

A grand loop starting from Guwahati, traversing the rain-soaked hills of Meghalaya, the rhino-protected plains of Kaziranga, and the spiritual heights of Arunachal Pradesh.

Day 1: Arrival in Guwahati and Transfer to Cherrapunjee

Arrive at Guwahati Airport or Railway Station and begin your journey to Cherrapunjee, known for its lush landscapes and heavy rainfall. En route, stop at the serene Umiam Lake, a man-made reservoir surrounded by rolling hills, ideal for photographs and relaxation. Check into your hotel in Cherrapunjee for an overnight stay.

Day 2: Cherrapunjee Sightseeing and Trek to Double Decker Living Root Bridge

After breakfast, explore Nohkalikai Falls, India’s tallest plunge waterfall, cascading dramatically into the lush valley below. Embark on a trek from Tyrna Village to the famous Double Decker Living Root Bridge in Nongriat, a marvel of natural engineering created over centuries by weaving rubber tree roots. Optional extension to Rainbow Falls is available for adventure enthusiasts. Return to your hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 3: Dawki Excursion and Transfer to Shillong

Visit Dawki, home to the crystal-clear Umngot River, where the water is so pure that boats appear to float mid-air. Optional water activities such as kayaking or boating are available. Travel to Shillong in the afternoon, checking into your hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 4: Shillong to Kaziranga National Park

After breakfast, drive to Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for the one-horned rhinoceros. Check into your hotel and spend the evening at leisure, soaking in the tranquil surroundings of this wildlife haven. Overnight stay in Kaziranga.

Day 5: Safari Adventure in Kaziranga

Start your day with an early morning elephant safari, offering a close encounter with rhinos, swamp deer, and exotic birds. Later, enjoy a jeep safari through the park to observe tigers, wild elephants, and diverse wildlife in their natural habitat. Return to the hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 6: Kaziranga to Bomdila | Scenic Drive

After breakfast, embark on a scenic drive to Bomdila, stopping for photography along rivers, forests, and hills. Explore local monasteries, including GontseRabgyaling Monastery and Thub-Chog Gatsel Ling Monastery, offering a peaceful spiritual experience. Overnight stay in Bomdila.

Day 7: Bomdila to Tawang | Through Sela Pass and Jaswant Garh War Memorial

Drive to Tawang, passing through the majestic Sela Pass and visiting Jaswant Garh War Memorial. Admire the stunning landscapes and alpine lakes en route. Check into your hotel in Tawang for overnight stay.

Day 8: Tawang Monastery and Local Cultural Tour

Visit the iconic Tawang Monastery, the largest in India, and explore Urgelling Gompa, the birthplace of the 6th Dalai Lama. Visit the War Memorial and local handicraft markets in the evening. Overnight stay in Tawang.

Day 9: High Altitude Lakes and Bumla Pass Exploration

Journey to Shonga-tser Lake (Madhuri Lake) and Sangetsar Lake, surrounded by pristine landscapes. Optional visit to Bumla Pass, located on the Indo-China border. Return to Tawang for overnight stay.

Day 10: Tawang to Dirang | Apple and Kiwi Farms

Drive to Dirang, stopping to explore local apple and kiwi orchards (seasonal). Visit Dirang Monastery and Kalachakra Monastery for a spiritual experience. Overnight stay in Dirang.

Day 11: Dirang to Bhalukpong | Tipi Orchidarium and Nag Temple

En route to Bhalukpong, stop at Tipi Orchidarium, showcasing over 500 orchid species. Visit the Nag Temple, reflecting the cultural heritage of local tribes. Overnight stay in Bhalukpong.

Day 12: Bhalukpong to Guwahati | Scenic River Views

Travel back to Guwahati along the Brahmaputra River, enjoying the serene riverine landscapes. Check into your hotel for an overnight stay. Optional evening activities include a river cruise or light-and-sound show.

Day 13: Kamakhya Temple & Departure

On the final day, visit the sacred Kamakhya Devi Temple, experiencing its spiritual ambiance and rituals. Transfer to Guwahati Airport or Railway Station for departure, concluding your 13-day journey across Northeast India.
